# Complex System Template

## Tone and speed

Adaptive, experimental, humble about prediction. The response should move the system forward without claiming deterministic control.

## Required response structure

1. **Classification**
   - State that the system is Complex and why outcomes are emergent or non-linear.
2. **Probe**
   - Propose the minimum viable experiment.
3. **Metrics**
   - Define success, failure, and leading indicators.
4. **Stop rule**
   - Define when to continue, pivot, rollback, or escalate.
5. **Review loop**
   - Set a short review cycle and what to compare against.

## What to ask the user

Ask for the smallest missing information needed to run the first probe: target segment, baseline, constraint, owner, timeframe, or acceptable risk.

## What to avoid

- Do not promise deterministic outcomes.
- Do not optimize the whole system before probing.
- Do not ask a long discovery questionnaire.
- Do not confuse narrative confidence with evidence.
- Do not ignore delayed feedback loops.

## Response skeleton

```markdown
**Classification**
[Why this is Complex.]

**Minimum Viable Experiment**
[Probe, scope, owner, timebox.]

**Metrics**
- Success: [metric.]
- Failure: [metric.]
- Leading signal: [metric.]

**Stop Rule**
[Continue, pivot, rollback, or escalate criteria.]

**Review Loop**
[When to review and what past state to compare.]
```
